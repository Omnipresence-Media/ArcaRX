# The ARCA Master Playbook
### A step-by-step curriculum for running your entire practice on one system

Written in plain English. No technical background needed. Work through it in order — each part builds on the one before it. Most people can learn a part in 15 to 30 minutes.

**How to use this guide:** Every lesson tells you three things — what the tool is, why you'd use it, and the exact clicks to do it. When you see words like "click" and "open," do them on your screen as you read. Learning by doing beats reading twice.

---

# Part 0 · The Basics

## Lesson 0.1 — What ARCA is
Think of your business today: one program for booking, another for charts, another for payments, a spreadsheet for memberships, a texting app for clients, maybe a marketing tool too. ARCA replaces all of them with **one system where everything talks to everything.**

There are two "sides" of ARCA, and you'll use both:

- **The Practice side** — what you and your staff see. Your calendar, your patients, your money, your marketing. This is your office.
- **The Client side (the Portal)** — what your patients or clients see on their phone. Their appointments, medications, results, and coaching. This is their experience of you.

One rule to remember: **anything you do on your side shows up correctly on their side.** Book a visit — they see it. Approve a photo — it's in their record. Enable a coaching program — it appears in their app.

## Lesson 0.2 — Getting in
1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Safari, or Edge).
2. Go to **arca-rx.vercel.app**.
3. Click **Log In** at the top right. You'll land on the **Command Center** — your home base.

## Lesson 0.3 — Finding your way around
Look at the left edge of the screen. That column is the **sidebar** — it's the hallway of your office. Every room is listed:

- **Front Desk** — Command Center, Calendar, Point of Sale
- **Patients** — Members, Memberships, Messages
- **Clinical** — AI Scribe, Charts/EMR, Protocols, Intake & Consent, Photo Reviews, Telehealth & RPM, Population Health
- **Analytics** — your numbers
- **Coaching** — the trainer/wellness side (Part 6)
- **Business tools** — inventory, billing, marketing, website

Two helpers to know from day one:
- **The search bar** (top middle) — type a patient name, a visit, an action. It's the fastest way anywhere. Keyboard shortcut: press **⌘K** (Mac) or **Ctrl+K** (Windows).
- **Coach AI** (top right) — your built-in assistant. Ask it questions when you're stuck.

**Try it now:** Click three rooms in the sidebar just to look. You can't break anything by looking.

# Part 1 · Front Desk — Your Day 1 Skills

## Lesson 1.1 — The Command Center (your morning coffee screen)
This is the first screen every morning. It answers: *how are we doing, and what needs my attention?*

Reading it top to bottom:
1. **The greeting bar** shows today's pace ("Practice is pacing +18.2% ahead of target").
2. **The alert banner** (peach colored) tells you if any high-risk patients need follow-up. Click **Review now** to see who.
3. **Four number cards**: Today's Revenue, Appointments, Active Members, Monthly Recurring. Green arrows mean growing.
4. **Revenue chart** — the last 30 days against your daily target line.
5. **Today's schedule** — who's coming in. Click **Chart** next to any name to open their file.
6. **Alerts** on the right — low inventory, overdue labs, unsigned notes.
7. The **location switcher** (top left, e.g. "Austin Flagship") — if you have more than one location, switch here.

## Lesson 1.2 — The Calendar (your appointment book)
Sidebar → **Calendar**. This replaces the paper book.

- Each **column is a provider**; each **row is an hour**. Colored blocks are appointments.
- Switch **Day/Week** with the toggle at top right. Move between days with the **‹ › arrows**; jump back with **Today**.
- The colored left edge of each block is its status: blue = confirmed, amber = checked in, green = in room, gray = done.

**To book an appointment:**
1. Click the dark **Book** button (top right) — or hover over any empty slot and click **+ Book**.
2. Pick the **service** (HRT consult, Botox, Lab draw…).
3. Pick the **provider** — or leave "First available."
4. Pick a **date and time** from the calendar of open slots.
5. Review the summary, click **Confirm appointment**. Done — it's on the calendar and in the patient's portal.

## Lesson 1.3 — Point of Sale (taking payment)
Sidebar → **Point of Sale**. Use it like a register:
1. Click **Add line** to add what you're selling (a service, a product, a package).
2. The subtotal, tax, and total calculate themselves.
3. Click **Charge $___**. Payment captured, receipt sent.

**Daily rhythm for front desk:** Command Center first thing → work the Calendar all day → POS at checkout. Those three screens are 80% of the job.

# Part 2 · Your Patients

## Lesson 2.1 — The patient list
Sidebar → **Members**. Every patient, searchable and filterable.
- Type in the **search box** to find anyone in seconds.
- Use the **filter chips** to see only HRT patients, only high-risk, only members, etc.
- Click **Add patient** (top right) — a small form opens: name, date of birth, contact, treatment track. Fill it and click **Add patient**.
- Click **Export** to download the roster as a spreadsheet.

## Lesson 2.2 — The patient chart (their whole story on one screen)
Click any patient's name. This is their file — the digital version of a thick paper folder:

- **Left card**: photo, tags, contact info, and three always-there buttons — **Book visit** (jumps to the calendar), **Charge/POS** (jumps to the register), and quick actions.
- **Overview tab**: their vital numbers (biomarkers), active protocols with a **+ Prescribe** button (opens a prescription form: medication, directions, quantity, refills, pharmacy — fill and **Send to pharmacy**), and recent visits.
- **Tabs across the top**: Encounters (visit notes), Labs, Prescriptions, Appointments, Invoices, Membership, Notes, Documents, Activity.
- **Membership tab**: see their plan; **Pause** or **Cancel** with confirmation. If they're not a member yet — **Enroll in membership**.

**Try it now:** Open any patient. Click through all the tabs once. Notice you never leave their file.

## Lesson 2.3 — Memberships (your recurring revenue)
Sidebar → **Memberships**. Plans like Platinum/Gold/Silver, member counts, and the MRR chart (monthly recurring revenue — money that arrives every month automatically).
- Click **New plan** to create one: name, monthly price, billing cadence, included perks → **Create plan**.

## Lesson 2.4 — Messages (texting, the professional way)
Sidebar → **Messages**. Two-way conversations with patients, all in one inbox.
1. Click a conversation on the left; read it on the right.
2. Type in the box at the bottom, press **Send**.
3. The phone and camera icons start a call or video visit. **View chart** opens their file mid-conversation.
4. **Bulk message** sends one announcement to a whole group.

# Part 3 · Clinical Tools

## Lesson 3.1 — The AI Scribe (your note-writer)
This is the feature providers love most. It **listens to the visit and writes the note for you.**

Sidebar → **AI Scribe**:
1. Pick the **patient** and **provider** from the two dropdowns.
2. Click the microphone to **start the visit**. Talk normally — the transcript scrolls live.
3. Click stop when done. In seconds, a complete **SOAP note** appears (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan — the four parts of a medical note), plus suggested diagnoses and lab orders.
4. Review it. Edit anything. Click **Regenerate** if you want a fresh draft.
5. Tick the checkboxes on suggested orders and click **Queue orders**.
6. Click **Push to chart**. The note is saved to the patient's file.

What used to take 15 minutes of typing after each visit now takes one review and two clicks.

## Lesson 3.2 — Charts / EMR
Sidebar → **Charts/EMR**. Recent visit notes, one click to read, **Edit** to change. **New encounter** offers to open the AI Scribe — say yes; it's the faster way to write a note.

## Lesson 3.3 — Protocols (your treatment recipes)
Sidebar → **Protocols**. A protocol is a standardized treatment path — like a recipe card for TRT, GLP-1, peptides.
- Click **+ New protocol**: name it, choose the track, write the dosing schedule and lab cadence → **Create protocol**. Now every provider follows the same playbook.

## Lesson 3.4 — Intake & Consent forms
Sidebar → **Intake & Consent**. Forms patients fill out before treatment.
- See what's **pending** vs **completed**.
- **Send** a form to a patient — it appears in their portal.
- **New form** opens a builder: name, type (intake/consent/photo release), start from a template → **Create form**.

## Lesson 3.5 — Photo Reviews (before & after)
Sidebar → **Photo Reviews**. Every before/after photo set in one review queue.
- Filter by status (Needs review / Approved) or protocol (Botox, Filler, HRT…).
- Click a card to open the detail panel: drag the **slider** to compare before/after, add notes, click **Approve**.
- The lock icon means no consent on file — get consent before using photos anywhere.
- **Upload photos** adds a new set.

## Lesson 3.6 — Telehealth & RPM (video visits)
Sidebar → **Telehealth & RPM**. Your virtual waiting room:
- Patients appear in the queue with status **Ready** or **Scheduled**.
- Click **Join now** to enter the video room. Inside: mute, camera, screen share, chat — and a notes panel where you can type the SOAP note during the call and **Save to chart**.
- **Pre-call** runs the checklist first (camera works, consent confirmed).
- The **RPM devices** and **Alerts** tabs show remote readings (blood pressure, weight) and flag anything out of range — click the action button on an alert to handle it.

## Lesson 3.7 — Population Health (no patient left behind)
Sidebar → **Population Health**. Instead of one patient at a time, this looks across *all* of them:
- **Registries** are groups: everyone with diabetes, everyone on hormone optimization. Click one to see its gaps.
- The **Care gaps worklist** lists exactly who is overdue for what ("A1c overdue 12 months"), with an action button on each row.
- **Run outreach** sends reminders to everyone with an open gap — one click, hundreds of patients nudged.
- **New registry** creates a new group: name, condition, inclusion criteria → **Create registry**.

# Part 4 · Getting Paid

## Lesson 4.1 — Inventory
Sidebar → **Inventory**. Every vial and product: stock levels, lot numbers, expiry, reorder alerts.
- When something runs low it flags itself; click **Reorder**.
- **Add SKU** (a SKU is just "a product you track"): name, category, units on hand, reorder point → **Add SKU**.

## Lesson 4.2 — Insurance & Billing (RCM)
Sidebar → **Billing/RCM**. If you bill insurance, this is mission control:
- **A/R** is money owed to you. **Denials** are claims insurance rejected — click **Appeal**, or select several and **Auto-appeal**.
- The **AI coding** tab suggests better billing codes visit by visit — **Accept** or **Reject** each one. Accepted codes mean money you'd have left on the table.
- **Submit batch** sends your claims; **Export 837** downloads the standard claims file.

## Lesson 4.3 — HIPAA & audit (Part 8 covers this fully)
Everything is logged automatically. You never have to do anything to stay compliant — but you can prove compliance anytime with one click (**Export log**).

# Part 5 · Growing the Practice

## Lesson 5.1 — Leads & Pipeline
Sidebar → **Leads**. Every inquiry that hasn't become a patient yet, organized by stage (New → Contacted → Consult → Won). Click **New lead**: name, contact, source, interest → **Add lead**.

## Lesson 5.2 — Email & SMS Automations (marketing while you sleep)
Sidebar → **Email/SMS**. Automations are messages that send themselves when something happens: a welcome text to every new lead, a follow-up after every visit, a birthday note.
- Each card shows an automation and its results (sent, open rate). **Pause/play** with one click. The pencil opens the editor.
- **New automation**: name, trigger (what starts it), channel (SMS/email), timing, message → **Create automation**.

## Lesson 5.3 — Reputation (your reviews)
Sidebar → **Reputation**. Your Google/Yelp ratings in one place.
- **Send review requests** texts your recent happy patients a review link — the single best way to climb Google.
- Reviews needing a response are flagged; click **Draft reply** and the AI writes a professional response for you to approve.

## Lesson 5.4 — Website
Sidebar → **Website**. Your public site, connected to your booking system. **Preview** shows changes; **Publish** makes them live; **Edit blocks** rearranges sections.

## Lesson 5.5 — Analytics
Sidebar → **Overview** (under Analytics). Revenue, acquisition funnel, channel performance. The one habit worth building: check **revenue vs target** weekly and **where new patients came from** monthly — it tells you where to spend.

# Part 6 · Coaching (the ARCA Pro side)

This section is for trainers, coaches, and any practice offering ongoing programs — and it's built around one big idea:

## Lesson 6.1 — The three programs
Every client can be given up to three kinds of program:
- **Fitness** — workouts and training plans
- **Health** — meal plans, macros, and diets
- **Protocol** — regimens: skincare routines, HRT dosing, GLP-1 schedules, supplements

**You decide which programs each client sees.** A weight-loss client might get Health + Protocol. An athlete might get only Fitness. Their app shows only what you switch on.

## Lesson 6.2 — Your clients
Sidebar → **Clients** (under Coaching). The roster with adherence scores and status (On track / At risk / Stalled). Click a client to open their profile:

- **Left card**: their goal, weight progress, adherence bar, and the three action buttons (Send check-in, Book session, Message).
- **The "Coaching programs" card** — this is the control panel. Three switches: **Fitness / Health / Protocol**. Flip one on and that program instantly appears in the client's app; flip it off and it disappears. The counter shows "2/3 on."
- **Preview client portal** — opens exactly what that client sees on their phone. Check it after changing toggles.

## Lesson 6.3 — The client profile tabs
- **Progress** — weight trend, before/after photos, personal records.
- **Results** — the Outcome Report (Lesson 6.7). Your proof.
- **Program** — their current training week, day by day.
- **Nutrition** — daily macros and 7-day adherence.
- **Protocol** — their assigned regimen: steps, supplements, dosing. **Send to client** delivers it; **Swap protocol** takes you to the library.
- **Check-ins** — their weekly numbers, mood, sleep, photos.
- **Messages** — your conversation.

## Lesson 6.4 — Building programs (Fitness)
- Sidebar → **Fitness** shows your program library (Cut Phase 12wk, Hypertrophy 8wk…). Click any program to see details; **Assign** links you to pick a client; **Duplicate** copies it.
- Sidebar → **Program Builder** is where you build: weeks across the top, sessions and exercises within each. **Publish** saves it to your library.

## Lesson 6.5 — Meal plans (Health)
Sidebar → **Health**. Plans with calories and macro targets. **New plan** opens the form (name, calories, protein/carbs/fat) → **Create plan**.

## Lesson 6.6 — The Protocol library
Sidebar → **Protocol**. Ready-made regimens — Brightening Skincare, Female HRT, GLP-1 Weight Management, Longevity — each with AM/PM steps, supplement stack, and dosing schedule. **New protocol** creates your own; **Assign to client** takes you to the roster.

## Lesson 6.7 — Outcome Reports (your proof, your sales tool)
Open any client → **Results** tab. One page combines their **before/after photos**, **weight trend**, **body composition changes**, and — the part nobody else can show — their **clinical markers** (A1c, cholesterol, resting heart rate, vitamin D) all improving.

Click **Open shareable report**. A clean page opens with **Print/PDF** and **Share** (copies a link the client can open, no login). Send it at re-sign time. Post it (with consent) when it's a win. This report is why clients stay.

## Lesson 6.8 — Running the coaching business
- **Packages & Billing** (sidebar) — your offers. **New package**: name, price, cadence, what's included → **Create package**. **Edit** on any card updates it.
- **Body Scans**, **Form Reviews** (watch a client's lift, annotate, **Send review**), **Challenges** (group competitions with leaderboards), **Video Library** (your exercise demos), **Coach Portal** (your public trainer page), **Client Messages**.

# Part 7 · What Your Clients See (the Portal)

Spend 10 minutes here — when a client calls confused, you'll know their screen.

## Lesson 7.1 — The patient portal
At **/portal** they see, top to bottom: a greeting with their care streak → their **next visit** (with **Reschedule** and **Join visit** buttons) → **today's medications** (they tap the circle to mark a dose taken) → **latest lab results** in plain language → **messages** from you → quick actions.

- **Visits**: they can **book** (same 4-step flow you use), reschedule, cancel, **join** a video visit, and read **notes** from past visits.
- **Join visit** opens a friendly "Ready to join?" screen, then the secure video call. One tap, no apps to install.
- **Meds, Labs, Progress, Billing, Shop, Account** — each is one tap in their menu.

## Lesson 7.2 — The coaching portal
At **/coaching** a coached client sees **only the programs you enabled**: their training week (tap a day to see exercises), their macros and meals, their protocol steps and supplements. If nothing is enabled yet, they see "Your coach will enable your programs soon."

# Part 8 · Safety & Compliance

## Lesson 8.1 — What's protecting you automatically
- Every record is **encrypted** (scrambled so only authorized people can read it).
- Every look at every chart is **logged** — who, what, when. That's the audit log.
- Patient consent is tracked (remember the lock icon in Photo Reviews).

## Lesson 8.2 — The HIPAA page
Sidebar → **HIPAA**. The audit log with **Export log** / **Export CSV** for inspectors, **access roles** (who on your team can see what — click **Edit** to adjust), and **compliance tasks** with due dates (**Resolve** / **Schedule** buttons).

**The one rule for staff:** never share your login. The audit log is only meaningful if everyone signs in as themselves.

# Part 9 · Your Routines (the actual playbook)

**Every morning (5 min):** Command Center → read the alert banner → scan today's schedule → check the alerts column.

**During the day:** Book from the Calendar. Check out through POS. Notes through the AI Scribe, same day. Reply to Messages between patients.

**Every Friday (20 min):** Photo Reviews queue to zero → Population Health: run outreach on open gaps → Reputation: send review requests → glance at revenue vs target.

**Monthly (30 min):** Analytics — where did new patients come from? → Memberships — is MRR growing? → Inventory — anything expiring? → Coaching clients — flip through Results tabs; send an Outcome Report to anyone up for renewal.

# Glossary (plain-English)

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| Portal | The app your patient/client sees |
| Chart / EMR | The patient's digital file |
| SOAP note | The standard 4-part visit note |
| Protocol | A standardized treatment recipe |
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue — subscription money |
| SKU | A product you track in inventory |
| A/R | Accounts receivable — money owed to you |
| Denial | An insurance claim that was rejected |
| Registry | A group of patients with something in common |
| Care gap | Something a patient is overdue for |
| Automation | A message that sends itself on a trigger |
| Adherence | How consistently a client follows their program |
| Outcome Report | The shareable proof-of-results page |
| RPM | Remote patient monitoring — home device readings |
| Audit log | The record of who viewed what, and when |
